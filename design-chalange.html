UX DESIGN CHALANGE

April 25, 2016

BY CRISTI FARCAS, FOR STRATA3

DESIGN BRIEF

On irishrail.ie, the tool which enables visitors of the site to search train times and fares and then reserve seats and pay for tickets is the most important element of the website for both the business owners and the end users.

That being the case it is crucial that this tool is as simple to use as it can be on all devices in order to ensure users intuitively flow through the process of planning a journey and buying train tickets. Your challenge is to review the end-to-end user flow of searching for a train time, selecting an outgoing and return train time, selecting the number of passengers, selecting seats and the subsequent steps up to the point of buying a train ticket and make design recommendations to improve the experience for users.

USER FLOW ANALYSYS

In preparing this report, I started by analysing and testing the online booking flow by myself. I then created a user journey map to clearly identify and illustrate all flow steps. Moreover, I researched service support conversations, for the last three months, on twitter, to discover any relevant difficulties users had faced during the booking process. The following problem kept my attention:


- Vague availability labels renders users confused in choosing a train.

- Repetitive information requests make users lose patience and feeling that the process is too long and complicated.

- Last but not least, users need an account in order to checkout. This required step makes the process considerably harder, taking the huge form to be completed, and opens the way to errors and discouraged buyers.

A NEW AND SIMPLIFIED
USER JOURNEY

Bellow, you can find a new user journey. The graphic illustrates a more linear and simplified booking process. In creating this flow, I used the following UX principles / criteria as guidelines:


- Reduce cognitive load by simplifying the requested user action per step ( have as few action per screen as possible)

- separate "booking flow" from "user acquisition": have a "visitor" option for uninterrupted booking

- in terms of "storytelling" have a more coherent, less fragmented, process
